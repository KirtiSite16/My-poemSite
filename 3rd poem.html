I Hate You, Because I Loved You Most

Theme: Conflicted hate born from deep love
I hate you, not with bitter flame, But with a love I couldn’t tame. A love so deep it cracked my core— And left behind a slamming door. I hated you when silence grew, When smiles were meant for someone new. You never saw what burned in me— Too blind to what you’d never see. I hate you still, this very day, For looking through me every way. For loving her with all your light, While I stood quiet, out of sight. I do not know if hate will fade, Or if this wound is meant to stay. I only know I gave you all— And watched myself begin to fall. You talk of her — your voice still sweet, But every word just brings defeat. Have you moved on? I’ll never know— But I can’t wait while time moves slow. I’m tired of aching in the grey, Of hoping love might look my way. So here I stand, no longer true— I hate you most, because I *loved* you.
